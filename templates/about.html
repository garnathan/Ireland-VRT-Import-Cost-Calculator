Understanding Vehicle Registration Tax and import charges for bringing vehicles to Ireland

What is VRT?

Vehicle Registration Tax (VRT) is a tax that must be paid when registering a vehicle in Ireland for the first time. This includes vehicles imported from other countries, including the UK.

VRT is calculated based on the vehicle's Open Market Value (OMV) and CO2 emissions, with rates ranging from 14% to 36% depending on environmental impact.

How Import Costs are Calculated
1. Open Market Value (OMV)

The OMV is the price a vehicle would fetch on the open market in Ireland, including:

  • Purchase price (converted to EUR)
  • Transport costs
  • Insurance during transport
  • Any other costs to bring the vehicle to Ireland
2. Customs Duty

Customs duty depends on the import origin:

From United Kingdom

Customs Duty: 10%

Applied to vehicle purchase price (excluding transport costs). This is an EU import duty for vehicles from non-EU countries (UK post-Brexit).

Applies to: England, Scotland, Wales
From Northern Ireland

Customs Duty: 0%

No customs duty applies as Northern Ireland remains part of the EU customs union for goods under the Northern Ireland Protocol.

Significant savings on import costs
3. Vehicle Registration Tax (VRT)

VRT rates are based on CO2 emissions. The amount payable is whichever is greater: the percentage of vehicle value OR the minimum amount.

CO2 Emissions (g/km) VRT Rate Minimum Amount Environmental Impact
0 - 50 7% €140 Very low emissions
51 - 80 9% €180 Low emissions
81 - 100 9.75% - 12% €195 - €240 Moderate emissions
101 - 120 12.75% - 16% €255 - €320 Moderate emissions
121 - 140 16.75% - 20% €335 - €400 Higher emissions
141 - 155 21.5% - 27.5% €430 - €550 High emissions
156 - 170 30% €600 Very high emissions
171 - 190 35% €700 Extremely high emissions
191+ 41% €820 Maximum rate

Important: VRT is calculated as whichever is greater - the percentage of the vehicle's value or the minimum amount shown above. This means:

  • Expensive vehicles typically pay the percentage rate
  • Lower-value vehicles may only pay the minimum amount
  • The system encourages importing lower-emission vehicles through significantly lower rates
Environmental Impact

The VRT system is designed to encourage environmentally friendly vehicle choices. A vehicle with 50g/km CO2 pays only 7% VRT, while a 200g/km vehicle pays 41% - nearly 6 times more!

4. Value Added Tax (VAT) - 21%

VAT is charged at 21% on the combined value of:

  • Vehicle purchase price
  • Customs duty
  • VRT amount

Note: VAT is calculated on the cumulative value, making it one of the largest components of the total cost.

5. Minimum VRT Amounts by CO2 Band

The VRT system uses detailed CO2 emission bands with specific minimum amounts. You pay whichever is greater: the percentage of vehicle value OR the minimum amount.

Low Emission Vehicles (0-100g/km)
  • 0-50g/km: 7% or €140 minimum
  • 51-80g/km: 9% or €180 minimum
  • 81-85g/km: 9.75% or €195 minimum
  • 86-90g/km: 10.5% or €210 minimum
  • 91-95g/km: 11.25% or €225 minimum
  • 96-100g/km: 12% or €240 minimum
Moderate Emission Vehicles (101-140g/km)
  • 101-105g/km: 12.75% or €255 minimum
  • 106-110g/km: 13.5% or €270 minimum
  • 111-115g/km: 15.25% or €305 minimum
  • 116-120g/km: 16% or €320 minimum
  • 121-125g/km: 16.75% or €335 minimum
  • 126-130g/km: 17.5% or €350 minimum
  • 131-135g/km: 19.25% or €385 minimum
  • 136-140g/km: 20% or €400 minimum
High Emission Vehicles (141-170g/km)
  • 141-145g/km: 21.5% or €430 minimum
  • 146-150g/km: 25% or €500 minimum
  • 151-155g/km: 27.5% or €550 minimum
  • 156-170g/km: 30% or €600 minimum
Very High Emission Vehicles (171g/km+)
  • 171-190g/km: 35% or €700 minimum
  • 191g/km+: 41% or €820 minimum
Highest rates apply to vehicles with very high emissions
Example Calculations with Official Rates
Example 1: Low Emission Vehicle

Vehicle: 2022 Hybrid, 85g/km CO2, £12,000

  • Vehicle Value: €14,040
  • Transport: €350
  • OMV: €14,390
  • Customs Duty (UK): €1,404
  • VRT (9.75%): €1,403 vs €195 min = €1,403
  • VAT Base: €16,847
  • VAT (21%): €3,538
  • Total: €19,487
Low emissions = lower VRT rate
Example 2: High Emission Vehicle

Vehicle: 2020 Petrol SUV, 180g/km CO2, £25,000

  • Vehicle Value: €29,250
  • Transport: €350
  • OMV: €29,600
  • Customs Duty (UK): €2,925
  • VRT (35%): €10,360 vs €700 min = €10,360
  • VAT Base: €42,535
  • VAT (21%): €8,932
  • Total: €51,817
High emissions = much higher costs
Key Insights from Official Rates:
  • Low emission vehicles (under 100g/km) have significantly lower VRT rates (7-12%)
  • Electric vehicles may qualify for even lower rates or exemptions
  • High emission vehicles (over 170g/km) face very high rates (35-41%)
  • The "whichever is greater" rule means expensive low-emission cars still pay percentage rates
  • Northern Ireland imports save the 10% customs duty, reducing total costs significantly
Import Process Steps
Before Import:
  1. Calculate estimated VRT costs
  2. Arrange transport to Ireland
  3. Get transit insurance
  4. Prepare required documentation
After Arrival:
  1. Declare import to Irish Customs
  2. Pay VRT within 30 days
  3. Register vehicle with NCTS
  4. Get NCT test (if required)
  5. Arrange Irish insurance
  6. Pay motor tax
Required Documents
For VRT Declaration:
  • Vehicle registration document
  • Purchase invoice/receipt
  • Insurance certificate
  • Valid ID (passport/driving licence)
For Registration:
  • VRT certificate
  • NCT certificate (if applicable)
  • Irish insurance certificate
  • Proof of address in Ireland
